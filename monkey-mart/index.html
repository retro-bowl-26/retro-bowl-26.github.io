🏪 What is Monkey Mart?

Monkey Mart is an arcade management game. The player controls a monkey moving around a top-down store. Walking over crops automatically harvests them into a stack. Walking over empty shelves automatically stocks them. Customers take the items and wait at the register to pay. Cash is used to buy new product stands, upgrade carrying capacity, and hire automated staff.

  • Automated gathering/stocking mechanics
  • Economic business progression
  • Staff management
  • Production chains (e.g., corn to chickens)

Pro Tips for Mastering Monkey Mart

📈 Prioritize Movement

When upgrading your character, prioritize movement speed over carrying capacity early on. Getting from the farm to the shelves faster is the biggest bottleneck.

🥚 The Production Chain

Animals need food to produce goods. You must manually harvest corn and feed it to the chickens before they will lay the eggs that your customers want.

💤 Staff Management

Hired workers are great, but they have a stamina bar. Once they fall asleep, production halts. Make it a habit to run laps around your store to bump into sleeping workers.

💰 Collect the Cash

Even with a cashier, money piles up on the floor next to the register. You still need to walk over the cash to actually add it to your bank account.

Tips and Strategies for Store Expansion

  • Hire Cashiers First: Standing at the cash register to collect money wastes valuable time. As soon as you can afford it, hire a monkey cashier so you can focus entirely on stocking shelves.
  • Upgrade Your Capacity: Upgrading your monkey's carrying capacity is crucial. Being able to carry 10 bunches of bananas at once instead of 4 drastically reduces your travel time.
  • Keep the Staff Awake: Your hired monkey assistants are lazy! They will occasionally fall asleep on the job. Make sure you walk past them to wake them up so your store keeps running efficiently.
  • Unlock New Products: Customers will eventually demand variety. Unlocking corn allows you to feed chickens to get eggs, and feeding cows gives you milk. These processed goods sell for much higher prices.
  • Don't Forget the Trash: Customers will occasionally throw peels and trash on the floor. Clean it up quickly, or other customers will slip, causing a delay in your sales.
