🐚 What is Jumping Shell?

Jumping Shell is a logic puzzle platformer. The player character consists of multiple layers of shells. The core mechanic allows the player to "shed" their outer shell at any time. This action propels the inner character upward (acting as a double jump) while leaving the outer shell behind as a solid physical object. Players use this to clear large gaps, block lasers, and hold down switches.

Pro Tips for Mastering Jumping Shell

🏗️ Staircase Building

You can stack your own discarded shells on top of each other to create a staircase to reach otherwise impossible heights.

🛡️ Laser Blocking

Your empty shells are completely solid. Drop a large shell directly in the path of a laser beam so your smaller character can safely walk past.

↩️ Re-entering Shells

You can jump back into a discarded shell to regain your larger size and double-jump ability. The puzzles often require you to retrieve a shell you used earlier.

⚖️ Weight Mechanics

Some buttons require more weight than others. Your tiny core character might not be heavy enough to press a large button, requiring you to drop a thick outer shell on it.

Tips and Strategies for Solving Puzzles

  • Think in Reverse: For harder levels, look at the exit door and work backward. Ask yourself, "Which shell needs to be where to open that door?" and plan your sequence accordingly.
  • The Shell Jump: Detaching from your shell while jumping acts as a double-jump. You can chain multiple shells together to scale massive walls.
  • Hold the Door: Your discarded shells retain physics. Use your larger outer shells to press down heavy floor switches while you navigate the smaller core character to the exit.
  • Size Matters: Some narrow corridors can only be entered by your smallest core character. Don't trap your large shell in a place where your small character can't retrieve it later.
  • Use the Reset: It's easy to soft-lock yourself by dropping a shell in a pit. Don't hesitate to hit the reset button the moment you realize your logic sequence was wrong.
