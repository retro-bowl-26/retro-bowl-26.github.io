Pro Tips for Mastering Slope

🧱 Moving Walls

When you encounter moving red walls, aim for the space where the wall *currently* is. By the time you reach it, the wall will have moved out of the way.

🌊 The Tunnel Vision

When entering narrow tunnels, try to stick to the floor. Riding high on the curved walls often leads to losing control when you exit the tunnel.

⏱️ Tap, Don't Hold

Because the physics are "floaty," holding a turn key builds up lateral momentum. Tapping ensures you don't oversteer into the abyss.

🔄 Accept Failure

You will crash frequently. Slope is designed to be punishing. Instantly restart and use the early slow sections to warm up your reflexes.

Tips and Strategies for High Scores

  • Stay Centered: Whenever possible, keep your ball in the middle of the track. Drifting too close to the edges leaves you with zero reaction time when a red block suddenly appears.
  • Look Ahead: Do not focus on your ball. Keep your eyes focused on the horizon of the track so you can anticipate upcoming tunnels, jumps, and moving walls.
  • Micro-Adjustments: At high speeds, holding the arrow keys for too long will send you flying off the map. Use rapid, light taps to make slight trajectory adjustments.
  • Master the Jumps: When flying off a ramp, try to land on flat surfaces. Hitting the side of a sloped wall upon landing will cause your ball to bounce unpredictably.
  • Embrace the Speed: The game gets incredibly fast after a score of 50. Don't panic; let your muscle memory take over and react purely to the gaps.
