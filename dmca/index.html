1. Commitment to Copyright Compliance

Retro Bowl 26 respects the intellectual property rights of game developers, publishers, and creators. We comply fully with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) and international copyright laws. Our platform operates primarily as an indexing portal, embedding HTML5 games hosted on third-party servers.

If you are a copyright owner (or an authorized agent) and believe that any content hosted or embedded on our site infringes upon your copyrights, we will act swiftly to remove or disable access to the material upon receipt of a valid DMCA notice.

2. How to File a DMCA Takedown Notice

To request the removal of copyrighted material, please send a formal written communication to our Designated DMCA Agent containing the following information:

  • Work Identification: A clear description or title of the copyrighted work that you claim has been infringed.
  • Infringing Material Location: The exact URL(s) on Retro Bowl 26 where the allegedly infringing game or asset is located.
  • Your Contact Details: Your full legal name, physical address, telephone number, and a valid email address.
  • Good Faith Statement: Include the exact phrase: "I have a good faith belief that the use of the copyrighted material described above is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law."
  • Perjury Statement: Include the exact phrase: "I swear, under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notification is accurate and that I am the copyright owner or am authorized to act on behalf of the owner of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed."
  • Signature: Your physical or electronic signature.

4. Counter-Notification Procedure

If a game developer or content provider believes their material was removed by mistake or misidentification, they may submit a Counter-Notice. The Counter-Notice must include:

  • Identification of the material that was removed and its previous URL.
  • A statement under penalty of perjury that the removal was a mistake.
  • The provider's name, address, and phone number.
  • Consent to the jurisdiction of the Federal District Court for the judicial district in which their address is located.
  • A physical or electronic signature.

5. Repeat Infringer Policy

In accordance with the DMCA, it is our policy to terminate partnerships with third-party sources or users who are deemed to be repeat infringers of intellectual property rights.
