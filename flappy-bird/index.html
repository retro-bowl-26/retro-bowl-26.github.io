🐦 What is Flappy Bird?

Flappy Bird is an endless arcade side-scroller. The player controls a bird that falls under gravity. Tapping the screen or pressing a button causes the bird to flap and rise slightly. The objective is to navigate the bird through an endless series of vertical pipes with gaps placed at random heights. Passing through a pipe earns one point. Colliding with a pipe or the ground ends the game instantly.

Pro Tips for Mastering Flappy Bird

⬇️ The Fast Drop

When transitioning from a very high gap to a very low gap, you must let gravity do the work. Stop tapping entirely the moment you clear the high pipe so you drop fast enough.

📏 The Hitbox Rule

The bird's beak extends slightly further forward than you think. Always leave a tiny bit of extra room between the front of the bird and the green pipes.

🔄 Embrace the Grind

A score of 10 is actually very good for beginners. Don't expect to hit 100 on your first day. The game requires developing highly specific muscle memory.

👁️ Screen Positioning

Keep your eyes focused on the right edge of the screen where the new pipes are appearing, using your peripheral vision to track the bird's actual height.

Tips and Strategies for Beating Your High Score

  • Find Your Rhythm: Do not panic-click. The key to Flappy Bird is establishing a steady, rhythmic tapping pattern to keep the bird hovering in a straight horizontal line before the pipes approach.
  • Aim for the Bottom Pipe: The bird's hitbox is slightly rectangular. It is generally safer to let the bird drop closer to the bottom pipe before flapping, rather than risking hitting your head on the top pipe.
  • Stay Calm: When your score reaches double digits, your heart rate will increase. Adrenaline makes your fingers twitchy, leading to early taps and immediate death. Breathe deeply and focus purely on the next gap.
  • Don't Look at the Score: Watching your score climb is a massive distraction. Focus your eyes entirely on the gap between the pipes on the right side of the screen.
  • The Double Tap Recovery: If you let the bird drop too far, a quick double-tap can save you, but you must immediately pause afterward to correct the massive upward momentum.
