🚦 What is Traffic Rush?

Traffic Rush is an arcade skill game. The player acts as a traffic director at a four-way intersection without stoplights. Vehicles spawn from all directions and drive straight. If two vehicles collide, the game is over. The player must intervene by stopping cars or speeding them up to ensure everyone crosses the intersection safely. The game gets progressively faster the longer you survive.

  • Two simple interaction mechanics

🎮 How to Play

  • 🖱️ Left Click (on car) Click a moving vehicle to make it brake and stop completely

Pro Tips for Mastering Traffic Rush

🚚 Slow Trucks

Large trucks move very slowly and block the intersection for a long time. It is almost always best to swipe them to speed them up and get them out of the way.

🛣️ The Safe Swipe

Only swipe a car if the entire intersection ahead of it is completely empty. A speeding car cannot be stopped in time if another car enters the cross-lane.

🛑 Don't Panic Click

If you see a crash about to happen, your instinct is to click rapidly. Take a deep breath and deliberately click the slower car to stop it while letting the faster one pass.

🎯 Peripheral Vision

Don't focus on the center of the intersection. Soften your focus and watch the four edges of the screen so you know what vehicles are approaching.

Tips and Strategies for High Scores

  • Keep Them Moving: It's tempting to stop every car, but that causes massive traffic jams. It is much better to swipe cars and speed them through the intersection to keep the lanes clear.
  • Watch the Motorcycles: Motorcycles move incredibly fast by default. It's usually best to let them pass on their own rather than trying to click and stop them, as they are hard to catch.
  • Clear the Intersections First: If you have multiple cars waiting at a red light, don't speed up a car from far away. Send the stopped cars through the intersection first to clear the space.
  • Listen to the Sounds: The game gives audio cues (like honking or engine revs) when vehicles approach at high speeds. Use this to anticipate danger before you even see the car.
  • Manage the Jams: You can stack stopped cars behind each other, but if a line of stopped cars stretches off the edge of the screen, you lose! You must keep the queue moving.
