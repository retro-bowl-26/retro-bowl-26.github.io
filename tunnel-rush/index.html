🌀 What is Tunnel Rush?

Tunnel Rush is a first-person 3D evasion game. The player moves forward automatically through an endless cylindrical tunnel at high speeds. The tunnel is filled with static and moving geometric hazards. The player must use the left and right directional keys to rotate along the inner wall of the tube to pass through safe gaps. The game is divided into levels, with speed and obstacle complexity increasing dramatically as you progress.

Pro Tips for Mastering Tunnel Rush

🛑 The Half-Pipe Trap

When you see a half-circle barrier blocking the bottom of the tunnel, immediately press and hold a direction key to ride up the wall and travel safely along the ceiling.

⏱️ Anticipate the Speed Boost

Every time you pass a level threshold, the tunnel briefly flashes and your speed increases instantly. Be prepared to react faster the moment the level counter ticks up.

⚖️ Center Yourself

After dodging a complex series of traps, try to return to what feels like the "bottom" or center of your screen. Being disoriented on the ceiling makes the next dodge much harder to calculate.

🔄 Take Breaks

The rapid visual changes and high speeds can cause eye strain. If you keep crashing on the same level, look away from the screen for 30 seconds to reset your vision.

Tips and Strategies for Surviving the Tunnel

  • Look Far Ahead: Do not stare at the obstacles directly in front of you; by the time you see them clearly, it's too late. Focus your vision deep into the center of the tunnel to spot the gaps forming in the distance.
  • Follow the Rotation: When approaching a spinning fan obstacle, strafe in the same direction the blades are rotating. This gives you slightly more time to slip through the gap.
  • Ignore the Color Changes: The game will aggressively flash and change the colors of the tunnel to disorient you. Train your brain to ignore the neon colors and focus purely on the solid shapes of the obstacles.
  • Micro-Movements: You don't need to spin wildly around the entire tunnel. Often, a tiny tap to the left or right is enough to slip past a barrier. Over-steering usually leads to crashing into the next wall.
  • Find the Flow State: Tunnel Rush is heavily rhythm-dependent. Relax your hands, don't grip the keyboard too tightly, and let your muscle memory take over when the speed becomes extreme.
